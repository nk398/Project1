Browsers Wars


Netscape soon found itself in a powerful position for the future of the web. Any design changes or new features included in Netscape Lake JavaScript frames cookies and others were soon copied by other browsers to stay compatible. In 1998 Netscape's dominance in the browser market became one of the central points in an anti-trust case against them. Microsoft's Internet Explorer during the early 2000s was incredible with near total control of the industry. Mozilla released version 1.0 of what was called phoenix meant to be a symbolic browser rising from the ashes of Netscape was completed in 2002

The name phoenix didn't stick though since Mozilla received complaints from the BIOS manufacturer phoenix technologies and in response the name changed to Mozilla firebird. Netscape market share had been all but decimated and right before being bought out by AOL then the incoming ISP landscape was made open source after several failed attempts to embed its user base AOL pulled the plug on that scape in 2008

At the same time of firebird's development, Apple had been working on a browser of their own based on the K HTML engine which was eventually worked into a new engine called webkinz. Google Chrome has risen to be the most popular web browser online today surpassing Firefox in early 2012 and IE a few months later. Crema comes for well over most of all web traffic and still appears to be growing. Google's massive web presence and its inclusion with the Android OS for mobile devices created a perfect storm that aren't even a 95% market share could save.
